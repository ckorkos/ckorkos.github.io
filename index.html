About Me

I discovered Human-Computer Interaction through a Cognitive Ethnography course at the University of California, San Diego. The class assigned students to observe people performing everyday tasks - everything from studying in public libraries to conducting music - and use the data to analyze the decision-making involved at each step. I'd always had an interest in psychology and cognitive science, and I was captivated by the practical application that these fields have in designing tools and systems. I will use my time in this profession to help people keep pace with the rapid advancement of technology in order to improve everyday life.
